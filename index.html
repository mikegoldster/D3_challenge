Correlations Discovered States' Average Age and Income

Based on U.S. Census Bureau Data, the above chart shows states by age and average income. No sharp trends can be observed from considering just these two factors: there are both high and average age states in both the upper and lower income tiers.

Generally, however, the youngest states (and D.C.) have above average income, perhaps indicating geographic mobility of opportunity-bound youth. The states with the lowest average incomes tend to have middle to high average age.
